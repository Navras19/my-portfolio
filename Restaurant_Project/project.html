About us

Discover Our History

Our journey started 15 years ago. Our restaurant, which has been serving Uzbek cuisine for 11 years, continues to serve both Original Uzbek cuisine and Persian cuisine for the last 10 years.

The chefs in our team consist of educated people with diplomas in their subjects.

We supply all our products directly from the producers daily, and we prepare our dough by hand in front of our customers.

We do not compromise traditional methods to reveal hidden flavors and use the highest quality, organic products for your health. If you want to discover new flavors, visit us, you won't regret it.

Authentic Uzbek Food
Get a taste of Sitora Restaurant and Discover the flavours and spices of the Silk Road

Rated(5.4)
Kazan Kebab

Marinated meat like mutton or beef that's pan-fried first with potatoes before being covered and steamed in a kazan at low heat. When fully cooked and tender, it's traditionally served with onions and fresh vegetables.

Rated(5.4)
Manti

These large steamed dumplings are typically made with spiced minced meat like ground lamb or beef, lamb fat, potatoes, onions, cabbage, pumpkin, and other vegetables.Like many Uzbek dishes, they're traditionally eaten by hand.

Rated(5.4)
Shashlik

Shashlik is traditionally made with lamb but it can be made with other types of meat as well like beef, chicken, and venison. Meat rolls made with ground beef or fatty beef (or lamb) are common, as are skewered and grilled chicken legs.

Rated(5.4)
Lagman

The hand-stretched noodles are boiled in water and served in a rich broth made with fried meat (usually lamb or beef), garlic, onions, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers, and other vegetables. It's typically seasoned with cumin seed, salt, and pepper before serving.

Rated(5.4)
Sambusa

Stuffed with a mixture of ground meat (usually lamb, beef, or chicken), tail fat, and spices. Meat samsa is the most common but you can find other versions as well like potato, pumpkin, or onion samsa.

Rated(5.4)
Shurpa

Uzbek soup traditionally made with lamb and thick slices of potato, carrot, onion, tomato, and sweet pepper. It's seasoned simply with salt and black pepper and often garnished with fresh dill, coriander, or parsley.

Rated(5.4)
Uzbek Plov

Uzbek plov is made with a base of grated and seasoned onions, carrots, and meat sautéed in fat. Rice is added before being topped with water and simmered until the rice is fully cooked.

Rated(5.4)
Tushpera (Chuchvara)

Being stuffed with a mixture of minced meat (usually lamb or beef), onions, and seasonings.Chuchvara are cooked in hot oil and typically served with a side of sour cream or cold yogurt.

Rated(5.4)
Omelette

Traditional uzbek dish Omellet with green onions in hot breakfast dish of eggs, milk and herbs

Rated(5.4)
Tukhm Barak - Egg Dumplings

Tukhm barak features a splendid combination of eggs and milk to generate its unique cheesy flavor.

Rated(5.4)
Bahor Spring Salad

Bahor -Spring Salad is so flavorful and vibrant! It's made of tomatoes, cucumber, red radishes, onions and fresh herbs.

Rated(5.4)
Non - Bread

In Uzbekistan, bread is prepared in tandoor ovens and has a deliciously smokey flavor. Additionally, you will notice that nons come in the form of a circle with a braided texture and a flat disc in the center.

Rated(5.4)
Sumalak

Sumalak is a sweet paste made from germinated wheat (young wheatgrass) and wheat flour, which is prepared especially for Nowruz (beginning of spring) in a large pot .

Rated(5.4)
Halva

This popular rich fudge-like dessert is made of sesame oil (or sunflower seed oil) and sugar syrup, vanilla bean, chopped almonds to cocoa powder and chocolate chips or pistachios .

Rated(5.4)
Parvarda

Parvada is a healthy candy made of sugar, flour, and lemon juice. There is no additive, preservative, or flavoring used in the preparation whatsoever. Therefore, you can feel confident this treat will not do you any harm.

Rated(5.4)
Nabot - Rock Candies

Nawat is a one-of-a-kind indigenous sweet made of mostly crystallized sugar. Besides its genuine and unique flavor, nawat also has positive impacts on the human body.

Rated(5.4)
Choy - Tea

Uzbekistan has a significant tea tradition. In this country, tea is more than simply a beverage. It is a cultural legacy. Tea is the first thing you receive from the host as a welcome gift when you enter his or her residence.

Rated(5.4)
Ayran - Salty Yogurt

It is best to enjoy this light and refreshing drink in the morning or before dinner. The drink will undoubtedly make your tastebuds ready for the main course.

Rated(5.4)
Sharbat

Sharbat is a sweet non-alcoholic drink. We have got variety of sharbats:
Almond Sharbat, Gulab(Rose) sharbat, Tamarind and Plum Sharbat.

Uzbek Wines

Don't miss opportunity to taste the best Uzbek wines. Pleasant odor and unusual sweet taste will make you feel the warmness of Uzbek sun. After the first sip you will feel the sun power inside you.
